Home Nucleus

Smart Home and Device Management App

Home Nucleus is an app that allows the user to centrally manage and control a number of different smart home devices from one location. Whether the user is just starting to use smart home devices or has a completely automated home, simplifying the control of your home increases the smart home experience.

See Prototype


Overview

Problem

Majority of users have smart home devices to make their lives easier and save time and effort. Yet, different smart home devices operate independently and do not always work well together. If you have multiple devices from different manufacturers, you’ll have to switch between multiple apps, which complicates the smart home experience.


Solution

The solution is one app that manages all of the multiple smart devices from one location to centralize the smart home management system. It eliminates the need for users to use multiple apps to manage their smart home, which creates a more convenient and effortless way to automate and experience the convenience of a truly connected home.

User Research

User Survey

A user survey was created to gather data on how smart home device users and non-smart home device users view smart home devices and the management of a smart home. Questions were asked to gather insight on how users use smart home devices and how they automate their home. Questions were also asked on how they manage their smart home experience.



Takeaway
Majority of users have smart home devices and use them to help save time and effort, but both, smart device users and non-smart home device users, had complaints on overall inefficiencies of managing a smart home.

Competitive Analysis

A SWOT analysis was performed on Samsung SmartThings, Wink Hub, and Logitech Harmony Hub. The purpose of selecting these competitors were to look at their products that attempt to centralize the management of smart home devices. It was also a great way of looking at these products to find where there were opportunities for a new product that could satisfy the market and focus on pain points that have not been addressed.



User Personas

User personas were developed to fully flesh out the goals and frustrations of our users. We created 3 personas, “The Ultimate Host” , “The Early Adopter”, and “The Environmentalist”. The main goals of each user persona was being able to automate their home in a convenient way and use smart home devices to make their lives easier. The main frustrations included difficulty operating multiple devices and smart homes requiring multiple apps and steps to automate.

User Flows

User Flows were developed by keeping the user’s pain points in mind. It was important to users that the app does not require an excessive number of steps to find commands or set up the smart home device.


Wireframes

First iterations of wireframes were rough sketches of different elements of the app. The second iteration of the wireframes were created in Figma. When building the user flows into screens, it was clear when there were missing components that needed to be added. By developing each concept, creating a prototype, and then testing the prototype, it helped me realize which process wasn’t fully developed and where we needed to redesign.



I conducted usability testing on the completed wireframe prototype. Redesigns from the testing results included adding confirmation messages, eliminating unnecessary fields, and adding a user guest mode to introduce the app.

Branding

Style Guide

Home Nucleus is defined by a modern and innovative brand identity. We want the visual design to represent modern technology, yet be approachable and include a homey personality . Since our users are of a wide demographic, we want to keep the design simple and functional. As a smart home management app, the design can be defined by 3 main principles: welcoming, contemporary, and minimalistic, which was conveyed in the color palette, typography choices, and logo.

Visual Design & Testing

Mockups & Iterations

The wireframes were used as the basis of creating the hi-fidelity mockups, also designed in Figma. After rounds of preference testing and feedback, the mockups were modified to include a simpler design by removing the logo on each screen, streamlining the dashboard, modifying the active state of the (+) bottom navigation to be clearer, and getting rid of the modal feature except for specific circumstances.



Hi-Fi Mockups Re-iterated

Three in-person usability tests were conducted on the Hi-Fi prototype to ensure that the flow of the screens were intuitive and inline with the brand of functional and simple.

A few changes were made from the results of the usability tests including: changing the color of the confirmation messages for clarity, increasing the spacing and look of the “Routine” screens for better content hierarchy, highlighting the text in Guest mode for emphasis, and adding an “are you sure” dialog box when removing a device to prevent user error. These edits were made to complete the Hi-Fi prototype.

Lessons Learned

This project reminded me that less was more. I constantly had to keep that in mind while I was iterating the mockups and the feedback from the preference and usability testing affirmed that simplicity was preferred. I learned that although certain design techniques and skills look visually appealing, it may not add anything to the user and in some cases, decrease the usability of the app. Testing was very important in these situations and reminded me that constant testing and iterating is a priority of any project.

If given more time, I would have loved to research more about the technology behind smart home devices and automating an entire smart home. More time would have given me an opportunity to better integrate the technical understanding into the interface and incorporate features that would contribute to a more robust user experience.
